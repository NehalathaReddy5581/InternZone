Company Research Guide

Use this checklist to prepare thoroughly for your next interview.

Company Overview

  • Full Company Name – Avoid mispronouncing it.
  • Industry & Sector – Tech? Healthcare? Finance? Know where they operate.
  • Founding Year & History – Who started it and why? Any major milestones?
  • Headquarters & Locations – Remote? Global offices? Expansion plans?
  • Subsidiaries & Parent Company – Does a larger company own them?
More →

Products, Services & Clients

  • What do they sell? – Products or services, and key offerings.
  • Who are their customers? – B2B (business clients) or B2C (individual consumers)?
  • Biggest clients? – Have they worked with major brands?
  • Pricing & Business Model – How do they make money?
  • Current Projects & Innovations – Are they launching anything new?
More →

Company Financials

  • Revenue & Profit – Growing or declining?
  • Stock Market Performance – If public, how has it been performing?
  • Funding & Investors – Any recent funding rounds (for startups)?
  • Debt & Stability – Are they financially healthy?
  • Recent Mergers & Acquisitions – Have they acquired other companies?
More →

Company Leadership & Key People

  • CEO & Executive Team – Their background, leadership style, and vision.
  • Hiring Manager & Team Leaders – Who you may be working under.
  • Board of Directors & Investors – Who has a say in major company decisions?
  • Company Culture from Leaders – What do executives post about on LinkedIn?
More →

Company Culture & Work

  • Core Values & Mission Statement – Align your answers with these!
  • Work-Life Balance – Does the company value flexible work?
  • Company Events & Employee Engagement – Do they invest in employees?
  • Diversity & Inclusion – Any major initiatives or issues?
  • Perks & Benefits – Remote work? Mental health support? Bonuses?
More →

Industry Trends & Market Position

  • Who are the competitors? – Know how they compare to others.
  • Market Trends Affecting the Company – Any risks or opportunities?
  • Regulations & Challenges in the Industry – Are they facing legal trouble?
  • Future Outlook – Expansion plans? New markets?
More →

Recent News & Public Perception

  • Latest Press Releases & Announcements – Any big news?
  • Social Media Reputation – What are people saying about them?
  • Customer Reviews & Complaints – Are clients happy or dissatisfied?
  • Recent Controversies or Scandals – Be prepared if this comes up.
  • Partnerships & Collaborations – Have they partnered with big names?
More →

The Job Role & Your Fit

  • Job Description Breakdown – Match your skills to their needs.
  • Who Will You Report To? – Find out the chain of command.
  • Growth & Promotion Opportunities – Is there career advancement?
  • Required Skills & How You Meet Them – Prepare examples for each.
  • Challenges of the Role – Identify potential roadblocks and how you’d handle them.
More →

The Interview Process & Expectations

  • Company’s Interview Style – Behavioral? Case study? Technical?
  • Common Questions Asked – Research previous candidate experiences.
  • What They Look for in Candidates – Skills, personality, or culture fit?
  • Dress Code & Company Atmosphere – Casual or formal?
  • Interviewer's Background – Look up their LinkedIn profile.
More →

Competitive Advantage & Strategy

  • Why They’re Better Than Competitors – Identify their strengths.
  • How They Plan to Grow – Are they expanding? New products?
  • Potential Threats & Weaknesses – Understand their challenges.
  • Your Unique Contribution – How you can help them succeed.
More →



HOW TO USE THIS INFO IN YOUR INTERVIEW



  • Craft Tailored Responses – Align your answers with company values.
  • Ask Smart Questions – Impress them with thoughtful insights.
  • Show Industry Knowledge – Mention trends and competitors.
  • Demonstrate Cultural Fit – Use what you learned about their values.
  • Highlight How You Solve Their Problems – Position yourself as a valuable asset.
